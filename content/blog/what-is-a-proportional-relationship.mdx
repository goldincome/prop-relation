You have 30 pencils and 10 erasers. Are the quantities proportional? How do you know?

This is the kind of question that trips up students — not because it's hard, but because "proportional" sounds more complicated than it is. Here's what it actually means.

## What Is a Proportional Relationship?

A proportional relationship is one where two quantities always change at the same rate. When one doubles, the other doubles. When one triples, the other triples. The ratio between them stays constant.

The math looks like this:

$$y = kx$$

Where $k$ is the **constant of proportionality** — the fixed rate that connects the two quantities.

## The Three Ways to Spot Proportional Relationships

Every proportional relationship can be identified three ways. If it passes all three, it's proportional.

### 1. Table: Constant Ratio

Take any two values in a table. Divide $y$ by $x$. If you get the same number every time, you've found a proportional relationship.

| $x$ | $y$ | $y \div x$ |
| :-: | :-: | :-: |
| 1 | 3 | 3 |
| 2 | 6 | 3 |
| 3 | 9 | 3 |
| 5 | 15 | 3 |

Every pair gives 3. That's $k = 3$, so $y = 3x$. Proportional.

Try a non-proportional table:

| $x$ | $y$ |
| :-: | :-: |
| 1 | 4 |
| 2 | 7 |
| 3 | 10 |

$4 \div 1 = 4, 7 \div 2 = 3.5, 10 \div 3 = 3.33$ — the ratios change. Not proportional.

### 2. Graph: Straight Line Through the Origin

A proportional graph is always a straight line that passes through $(0, 0)$. Every time.

If the line is straight but starts at $(0, 2)$ instead of $(0, 0)$, it's not proportional — even though it looks similar.

### 3. Equation: $y = kx$ Form

If you can write the relationship as $y = kx$ with no extra constant added or subtracted, it's proportional.

- $y = 5x$ — proportional ($k = 5$)
- $y = \frac{2}{3}x$ — proportional ($k = \frac{2}{3}$)
- $y = 4x + 1$ — not proportional (the $+ 1$ breaks it)

## Real-Life Examples

**Buying gas.** If gas costs \$4 per gallon, the total cost is $y = 4x$ where $x$ is gallons. Double the gas, double the cost. That's proportional.

**Walking speed.** You walk 3 miles per hour. Distance $y = 3x$ where $x$ is hours. Two hours = 6 miles. Four hours = 12 miles. Proportional.

**Pizza delivery fee.** A pizza costs \$10 plus a \$5 delivery fee. Total $y = 10x + 5$. Order two pizzas and the fee doesn't double. Not proportional.

## Common Mistakes

- **Thinking "straight line" is enough.** A straight line that doesn't go through the origin is *not* proportional.
- **Dividing wrong.** Always divide $y$ by $x$, not $x$ by $y$.
- **Forgetting $x = 0$.** In a proportional relationship, when $x = 0$, $y$ must be 0.

## FAQ

**What is a proportional relationship in simple terms?**
It means two quantities rise and fall together at the same rate. The ratio $y \div x$ is always the same number.

**What is the constant of proportionality?**
It's the fixed number $k$ in $y = kx$. It tells you how much $y$ changes when $x$ changes by 1.

**Can a proportional relationship have negative numbers?**
Yes. If $k$ is negative, one quantity increases while the other decreases. The ratio $y \div x$ still stays constant.

**What's the difference between proportional and equivalent?**
Equivalent ratios are ratios that simplify to the same value. A proportional relationship is any relationship where all ratios are equivalent.

**Do all proportional relationships start at zero?**
Yes. When $x = 0$, $y$ must equal 0 in a proportional relationship like $y = kx$.
