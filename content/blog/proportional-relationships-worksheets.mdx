## Table Practice

Students compute $y \div x$ for each row to determine proportionality.

**Sample Problem:** Table A has pairs $(3, 12)$, $(5, 20)$, $(7, 28)$. Table B has $(1, 6)$, $(3, 10)$, $(5, 14)$. Which is proportional?

**Solution:** Table A: $12 \div 3 = 4$, $20 \div 5 = 4$, $28 \div 7 = 4$. $k = 4$. $y = 4x$. Proportional. Table B: ratios differ. Not proportional.

## Graph Practice

Students check two conditions: straight line? passes through $(0, 0)$?

**Sample Problem:** Graph A goes through $(0, 0)$, $(2, 8)$, $(5, 20)$. Graph B goes through $(0, 3)$, $(2, 7)$, $(4, 11)$. Which is proportional?

**Solution:** Graph A: $k = 8 \div 2 = 4$, through origin. Proportional. Graph B: doesn't pass through $(0, 0)$. Not proportional.

## Equation Practice

Students classify equations as proportional or not.

**Sample Problem:**
1. $y = 6x$ — proportional? $k$?
2. $y = \frac{3}{4}x + 2$ — proportional?
3. $y = 0.2x$ — proportional? $k$?

**Answers:** 1. Yes, $k = 6$. 2. No. 3. Yes, $k = 0.2$.

## Word Problems

Twelve real-world scenarios requiring students to extract the rate and write $y = kx$.

**Sample Problem:** A faucet drips at a constant rate. After 15 minutes, 45 mL have leaked. After 25 minutes, 75 mL. Write the equation.

**Solution:** $k = 45 \div 15 = 3$. Check: $75 \div 25 = 3$. $y = 3x$ ($y$: mL, $x$: minutes).
