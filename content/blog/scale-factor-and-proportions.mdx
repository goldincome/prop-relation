You take a 4 &times; 6 inch photo and enlarge it to 8 &times; 12 inches. Every dimension doubled. The enlargement still looks exactly right — just bigger. That "2" you multiplied by? That's the **scale factor**.

## What Is Scale Factor?

Scale factor ($k$) is the number you multiply each dimension of a figure by to produce a similar figure. Similar figures have the same shape but different size. Their corresponding angles are equal, and their corresponding sides are proportional.

$$k = \frac{\text{new length}}{\text{original length}}$$

If $k > 1$, you get an **enlargement**. If $0 < k < 1$, you get a **reduction**.

| Scale Factor | Effect | Example |
| :-: | :-: | :-: |
| $k = 2$ | Enlargement (doubles) | 4 &times; 6 → 8 &times; 12 |
| $k = \frac{1}{2}$ | Reduction (halves) | 4 &times; 6 → 2 &times; 3 |
| $k = 1$ | Congruent (same size) | No change |
| $k = 3$ | Enlargement (triples) | 4 &times; 6 → 12 &times; 18 |

## Scale Factor Is the Constant of Proportionality

When two figures are similar, every pair of corresponding sides forms a proportional relationship. The scale factor $k$ is the constant of proportionality that connects them.

If triangle A has sides 3, 4, 5 and triangle B (similar) has sides 6, 8, 10:

$$\frac{6}{3} = 2, \quad \frac{8}{4} = 2, \quad \frac{10}{5} = 2$$

$$k = 2$$

Every side of A multiplied by $k = 2$ gives the corresponding side of B. This is the same $k$ you see in $y = kx$ — just applied to geometry.

## How to Find the Scale Factor Between Two Figures

**Step 1:** Identify a pair of corresponding sides.
**Step 2:** Divide the length in the new figure by the length in the original figure.
**Step 3:** Simplify.

> A rectangle is 5 cm wide in the original and 15 cm wide in the enlargement.

$$k = \frac{15}{5} = 3$$

The scale factor is 3. Every dimension tripled.

> A building is 40 m tall in a blueprint drawing scaled to 10 cm.

$$k = \frac{10}{40} = \frac{1}{4}$$

The scale factor is $\frac{1}{4}$. One centimeter represents 4 meters.

## Using Scale Factor to Find Missing Sides

If you know the scale factor, multiply the original side by $k$ to find the new side.

| Original Side | $k$ | New Side |
| :-: | :-: | :-: |
| 7 cm | 4 | $7 \times 4 = 28$ cm |
| 12 in | $\frac{1}{3}$ | $12 \times \frac{1}{3} = 4$ in |
| 2.5 m | 2.5 | $2.5 \times 2.5 = 6.25$ m |
| 9 ft | $\frac{2}{3}$ | $9 \times \frac{2}{3} = 6$ ft |

To find the original side from the new side, divide by $k$:

$$\text{original} = \frac{\text{new}}{k}$$

## Scale Factor and Area

Scale factor affects area differently. When dimensions scale by $k$, area scales by $k^2$.

A 3 &times; 5 rectangle has area $15$ square units. Scale by $k = 2$: dimensions become 6 &times; 10, area becomes $60$ square units.

$$\frac{60}{15} = 4 = 2^2$$

Double the sides, quadruple the area. This matters when painting, flooring, or tiling scaled designs.

## Real-World Uses of Scale Factor

| Context | How Scale Factor Works |
|---|---|
| **Maps** | A 1 : 100,000 scale means $k = \frac{1}{100,000}$ |
| **Blueprints** | 1 cm on paper = 50 cm in reality ($k = 50$) |
| **Model cars** | 1 : 24 scale means the model is $\frac{1}{24}$ the real size |
| **Photo printing** | Resize a 4 &times; 6 to 8 &times; 12 using $k = 2$ |
| **Architecture** | Scaled floor plans preserve room proportions |

A map scale of 1 : 50,000 means every centimeter on the map represents 50,000 centimeters (0.5 km) on the ground. That ratio _is_ a proportion.

## Practice Problems

**Problem 1:** A triangle has sides 6 cm, 8 cm, and 10 cm. A similar triangle has sides 15 cm, 20 cm, and 25 cm. Find the scale factor.

$$k = \frac{15}{6} = 2.5$$

Check: $8 \times 2.5 = 20$, $10 \times 2.5 = 25$. Correct.

**Problem 2:** A 5 &times; 7 photo is reduced by a scale factor of $k = 0.4$. What are the new dimensions?

$$5 \times 0.4 = 2 \text{ inches}$$
$$7 \times 0.4 = 2.8 \text{ inches}$$

The reduced photo is 2 &times; 2.8 inches.

**Problem 3:** Two similar rectangles have areas of 12 sq cm and 108 sq cm. Find the scale factor.

Area scales by $k^2$:

$$k^2 = \frac{108}{12} = 9$$

$$k = \sqrt{9} = 3$$

The scale factor is 3.

## FAQ

**What is the difference between scale factor and ratio?**
A ratio compares two quantities. A scale factor is a specific ratio that multiplies every dimension of a figure to produce a similar figure. All scale factors are ratios, but not all ratios are scale factors.

**Can scale factor be less than 1?**
Yes. When $0 < k < 1$, you get a reduction. The new figure is smaller than the original but still has the same shape.

**What does a scale factor of 1 mean?**
The figures are congruent — identical in size and shape. Zero change.

**How is scale factor related to the constant of proportionality?**
They are the same idea. In geometry, scale factor is the constant of proportionality ($k$) between corresponding side lengths of similar figures. The equation $y = kx$ still applies: new side = $k \times$ original side.

**Does scale factor affect angles?**
No. Corresponding angles in similar figures are always equal regardless of scale factor. Shape stays the same; only size changes.

**How do you find scale factor when given areas?**
Area scales by $k^2$. Divide new area by original area, then take the square root: $k = \sqrt{A_\text{new} \div A_\text{original}}$.

**What is the difference between scale factor and scale?**
"Scale" is the ratio shown on a map or blueprint (like 1 : 100). "Scale factor" is the actual multiplier you apply to lengths. Scale factor = new length ÷ original length.

**How do you check if two figures are similar?**
Verify that all corresponding angles are equal and all corresponding sides are proportional with the same scale factor. If even one pair of sides uses a different $k$, the figures are not similar.
