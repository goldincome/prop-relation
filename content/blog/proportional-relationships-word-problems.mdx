A recipe calls for 3 cups of flour for every 2 batches of cookies. You need to make 5 batches. How much flour do you need?

Most students know they should "do something with 3, 2, and 5." The trouble is figuring out what. This article gives you a repeatable method so you never guess again.

## The Four-Step Method

Every proportional word problem follows the same structure. Learn these four steps and you can solve any of them.

**Step 1: Identify the two quantities.** Figure out what's being compared. These become your $x$ and $y$.

**Step 2: Set up the ratio.** Write the known relationship as $\frac{y}{x} = k$.

**Step 3: Cross-multiply.** Substitute the value you know and solve for the one you don't.

**Step 4: Check your units.** The answer should make sense in the real world.

Let's apply it.

## Speed Problems: Distance = Rate $\times$ Time

**A car travels 180 miles in 3 hours at a constant speed. How far will it travel in 5 hours?**

**Step 1:** Quantities are distance (miles) and time (hours). Distance depends on time, so $y =$ distance, $x =$ time.

**Step 2:** Find the unit rate.

$$k = \frac{180}{3} = 60 \text{ miles per hour}$$

**Step 3:** Write the equation and solve.

$$y = 60x$$

$$y = 60(5) = 300$$

**Step 4:** 300 miles in 5 hours at 60 mph — makes sense.

| Time (hours) | Distance (miles) |
| :-: | :-: |
| 1 | 60 |
| 3 | 180 |
| 5 | 300 |

The proportion $\frac{180}{3} = \frac{300}{5}$ holds because both ratios equal 60.

## Shopping and Unit Price Problems

**A store sells 8 ounces of cheese for \$5.20. How much would 13 ounces cost at the same rate?**

**Step 1:** Quantities are cost (dollars) and weight (ounces). Cost depends on weight.

**Step 2:** Find the unit price.

$$k = \frac{5.20}{8} = 0.65$$

Each ounce costs \$0.65.

**Step 3:** Multiply.

$$y = 0.65(13) = 8.45$$

**Step 4:** \$8.45 for 13 ounces — about \$0.65 per ounce, checks out.

The equation $y = 0.65x$ means any weight $x$ costs \$0.65 per ounce. You can also solve this as a proportion:

$$\frac{5.20}{8} = \frac{y}{13}$$

Cross-multiply: $5.20 \times 13 = 8y$, then $y = \frac{67.6}{8} = 8.45$.

## Recipe Scaling Problems

**A pancake recipe uses 2 eggs for every 3 cups of flour. To make a larger batch, you use 9 cups of flour. How many eggs do you need?**

**Step 1:** Quantities are eggs and cups of flour.

**Step 2:** The ratio $\frac{\text{eggs}}{\text{flour}} = \frac{2}{3}$ is the constant.

$$k = \frac{2}{3}$$

**Step 3:** $y = \frac{2}{3}x$, where $y$ is eggs and $x$ is flour.

$$y = \frac{2}{3}(9) = 6$$

**Step 4:** 6 eggs for 9 cups — triple the original recipe, triple the eggs. Correct.

Check: $\frac{2}{3} = \frac{6}{9}$. Both simplify to the same ratio.

## Map and Scale Problems

**On a map, 1 inch represents 25 miles. Two cities are 3.5 inches apart on the map. How far apart are they in real life?**

**Step 1:** Quantities are map distance (inches) and real distance (miles).

**Step 2:** $k = \frac{25}{1} = 25$ miles per inch.

**Step 3:** $y = 25x$
$$y = 25(3.5) = 87.5$$

**Step 4:** 87.5 miles for 3.5 inches at 25 miles per inch — correct.

| Map Distance (in) | Real Distance (mi) |
| :-: | :-: |
| 1 | 25 |
| 3.5 | 87.5 |

The proportion $\frac{1}{25} = \frac{3.5}{87.5}$ holds. Cross-multiply: $1 \times 87.5 = 25 \times 3.5$.

## Common Mistakes in Word Problems

**Mixing up which quantity is $x$ and which is $y$.** In the speed problem, if you set $y =$ time and $x =$ distance, $k = \frac{3}{180} = \frac{1}{60}$. The math still works but the units are awkward. Keep $y$ as the dependent quantity (what you're solving for).

**Adding instead of multiplying.** "3 cups for 2 batches, so 5 batches needs 3 + 2 + 5 = 10 cups." No. The relationship is multiplicative: $\frac{3}{2} = \frac{y}{5}$.

**Using the wrong pair in a proportion.** If 3 inches on a map equals 75 miles, and you need to find what 5 inches equals, don't set up $\frac{3}{5} = \frac{x}{75}$. The correct setup is $\frac{3}{75} = \frac{5}{x}$.

**Forgetting to check for reasonableness.** If cheese costs \$5.20 for 8 ounces, 13 ounces should cost *more* than \$5.20. If your answer is \$3.40, you divided instead of multiplied.

## FAQ

**How do I know which quantity goes on top in a proportion?**
Put the quantity you're solving for on top of one side, then mirror the unit on the other side. If solving for cost, put cost on top in both ratios.

**What if the numbers aren't neat round numbers?**
Same method. $k = \frac{12.75}{3} = 4.25$ works exactly like $k = \frac{180}{3} = 60$. Fractions and decimals don't change the steps.

**Can I use cross-multiplication instead of finding $k$?**
Yes. Setting up $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}$ and cross-multiplying $a \times d = b \times c$ is the same as finding the unit rate and scaling up. Use whichever you're more comfortable with.

**How do I check if a word problem is proportional in the first place?**
Ask: does doubling one quantity double the other? If yes, it's proportional. Speed, unit price, simple recipes, and map scales are almost always proportional.

**What if there's a starting fee?**
If the problem says "a \$5 base fee plus \$10 per hour," that's $y = 10x + 5$. Not proportional — the +5 breaks it. Look for "per," "each," or "every" without extra fees.

**Do I always need to write the equation?**
No. Many students solve using only proportions: $\frac{3}{2} = \frac{x}{5}$. But writing $y = kx$ helps you see the structure, especially on multi-step problems.

**What about inverse proportion word problems?**
Those use $y = \frac{k}{x}$, not $y = kx$. If doubling one quantity *halves* the other, it's inverse. That's a different class of problem.

**My teacher says to find the unit rate first. Is that always necessary?**
It's the most reliable method. Finding $k$ first means you always know the single step that connects any two values. You can solve any $x$ or $y$ with one multiplication or division.

## Practice Problems

Try these on your own before checking the answers.

**Problem 1:** A bike travels 45 miles in 3 hours at a constant speed. How far will it travel in 7 hours?

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  
  $k = \frac{45}{3} = 15$ mph. $y = 15(7) = 105$ miles.
</details>

**Problem 2:** 6 apples cost \$3.90 at the farmer's market. How much would 10 apples cost at the same rate?

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  
  $k = \frac{3.90}{6} = 0.65$ per apple. $y = 0.65(10) = \$6.50$.
</details>

**Problem 3:** A map uses a scale of 0.5 inches = 20 miles. Two towns are 2.75 inches apart on the map. How far apart are they?

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  
  $k = \frac{20}{0.5} = 40$ miles per inch. $y = 40(2.75) = 110$ miles.
</details>

**Problem 4:** A cookie recipe needs 4 cups of flour for 3 batches. You want to make 8 batches. How much flour do you need?

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  
  $k = \frac{4}{3}$ cups per batch. $y = \frac{4}{3}(8) = \frac{32}{3} = 10\frac{2}{3}$ cups.
</details>
