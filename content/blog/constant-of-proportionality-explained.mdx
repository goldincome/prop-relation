If a car travels 60 miles in 1 hour, 120 miles in 2 hours, and 180 miles in 3 hours, something stays the same the whole time: the number 60. That number — the constant rate — is the **constant of proportionality**.

## What Is the Constant of Proportionality?

The constant of proportionality ($k$) is the fixed number that relates two quantities in a proportional relationship. Every proportional relationship has exactly one $k$.

The equation is:

$$y = kx$$

- $y$ depends on $x$
- $k$ is the constant multiplier
- $x$ is the independent variable

If you know $k$, you can predict any $y$ from any $x$.

## How to Find $k$

There are four common ways to find the constant of proportionality.

### 1. From a Table

Divide $y$ by $x$ for any row. The result is $k$.

| $x$ | $y$ |
| :-: | :-: |
| 3 | 15 |
| 7 | 35 |
| 10 | 50 |

$$k = \frac{15}{3} = 5, \quad \frac{35}{7} = 5, \quad \frac{50}{10} = 5$$

$$k = 5$$

### 2. From a Graph

Pick any point on the line where $x \neq 0$. Divide the $y$-coordinate by the $x$-coordinate.

If a line passes through $(4, 12)$:

$$k = \frac{12}{4} = 3$$

The line is $y = 3x$.

### 3. From an Equation

If the equation is in $y = kx$ form, $k$ is the coefficient of $x$.

- $y = 7x$ → $k = 7$
- $y = \frac{2}{5}x$ → $k = \frac{2}{5}$
- $y = 0.25x$ → $k = 0.25$

### 4. From a Word Problem

Identify the two quantities and divide the dependent quantity by the independent quantity.

> A pool fills at 8 gallons per minute.

$$k = 8 \text{ gallons per minute}$$

The equation is $y = 8x$, where $y$ is gallons and $x$ is minutes.

## What $k$ Means in Real Life

$k$ always has a unit. It describes how much $y$ changes per 1 unit of $x$.

| Situation | $k$ | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| Earning \$15/hour | 15 | You earn \$15 per hour worked |
| 3 cups of flour per batch | 3 | Each batch needs 3 cups |
| Reading 2 pages per minute | 2 | Each minute you read 2 pages |
| \$0.25 per ounce | 0.25 | Each ounce costs 25 cents |

$k$ converts "how many units of $x$" into "how many units of $y$."

## Common Mistakes

**Dividing $x$ by $y$ instead of $y$ by $x$.** Check your units. If $y$ is dollars and $x$ is hours, $k$ should be dollars per hour — so divide dollars by hours.

**Forgetting $k$ can be a fraction.** $k = \frac{2}{3}$ is perfectly valid. Not every constant is a whole number.

**Assuming $k$ is always positive.** A negative $k$ means $y$ decreases as $x$ increases. The relationship is still proportional.

## FAQ

**What is the constant of proportionality called in geometry?**
In geometry, $k$ is often called the scale factor when comparing similar figures.

**Is the constant of proportionality the same as slope?**
Yes, when the line passes through the origin. In a proportional relationship $y = kx$, $k$ is both the constant of proportionality and the slope.

**Can $k$ be zero?**
Technically yes: $y = 0x = 0$. But this is a trivial case that rarely appears in 7th-grade problems.

**How do you find $k$ in a graph with a curved line?**
You can't. Curved lines don't represent proportional relationships, so they don't have a single constant of proportionality.

**What if $k$ changes after the first row?**
Then the table doesn't represent a proportional relationship. The constant must be the same for every pair.
